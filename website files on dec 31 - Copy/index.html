What is a derivative?

A function's derivative is an equation for the slope of that function at any point.
If you want to find the slope of a function at a certain point, you can derive the function and plug in the certain point's x value into the derivative for x. The y value it produces will be the slope at that point.

What is an integral?

An indefinite integral (antiderivative) is an equation received after applying derivative rules but reversed.
An indefinite integral is notated as a capital letter (e.g. F(x) is the indefinite integral of f(x), which means f(x) is the derivative of F(x)).
Not all functions have an indefinite integral.
A definite integral is a number representing the area between a line and the x axis (area under a curve). This value can be negative.

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Essentially, the value of a definite integral is the difference of the antiderivative evaluated at each endpoint.

What is a limit?

A limit is defined as the value that f(x) approaches as the input values approach a.

A limit only exists when the limit from the left and limit from the right are equal.

Limit Rules






How to Solve a Limit

Try direct substitution.

   If the answer is defined, the evaluation is the limit.

   If the answer is zero divided by zero, rewrite the limit. Try factoring , conjugates , or trig identities . Then try direct substitution again.

   If the answer is some number divided by zero, you probably have an asymptote. Graph or plug in numbers from the left or right to see if the limit is positive or negative infinity.

   If the evaluation is still zero divided by zero after rewriting, try graphing and tables to approximate the limit.

Example:

x-y pairs as x approaches 1 from the left:


0.9 , 1.9

0.99 , 1.99

0.999 , 1.999


x-y pairs as x approaches 1 from the right:


1.001 , 2.001

1.01 , 2.01

1.1 , 2.1


From the left, as x approaches 1, f(x) approaches 2.

From the right, as x approaches 1, f(x) also approaches 2.

Since these are equal, the answer to the limit is 2 even though the function is undefined at 2.

What are parametric equations?

Parametric equations are functions linked by a common independent variable.

Usually, parametric equations will have an x and y equation linked with a t  variable (time).

As t  varies over a certain range, the equations will produce x-y pairs.

Parametric equations are convenient for graphing curves.

What is Euler's Method?

Euler's method is a technique used for approximation using multiple tangent lines.

Essentially, the logic behind this is that if the first point and derivative is given, one can repeatedly draw tangent lines over some distance to get an approximation of a y value.

What are vectors?

Vectors are ordered pairs of real numbers (like parametric equations) containing a direction and magnitude.

They are represented in component form as <a,b>

Vectors are represented by an arrow from the origin to the point.

The magnitude is represented by the length of the arrow (use Pythagoream's Theorem).

The direction is the smallest positive angle between the positive x-axis and the arrow representation.
